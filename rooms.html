Things worth knowing before you search

Hoegi Station is the direct route

Line 1 stops right there, and Gyeongui-Jungang and Gyeongchun lines also serve it. KHU is 15 minutes on foot or one Dongdaemun-01 bus ride. Living near Hoegi keeps the commute under 20 minutes.

Line 6 always means a transfer

Seokgye Station is where Line 6 meets Line 1. Any Line-6 neighbourhood (Anam, Bomun, Wolgok) routes through there. Budget 40–50 minutes total. Worth it if the rent saves ₩200k+ per month.

Keep the deposit small

Without an ARC (Alien Registration Card, issued after arrival) many landlords ask for a bigger deposit. 33m2 (₩330k fixed), Ziptoss English contracts, and LiveAnywhere escrow are the safest low-deposit routes.

Short-stay platforms cost more per month

Fully furnished, bills-included short-stay units run a premium. If a ₩5M deposit is manageable, a standard studio via Ziptoss with a negotiated 4–6 month contract is often cheaper overall.

Pay through the platform only

Never wire money directly to a landlord before a signed contract. Use the platform’s payment flow or escrow. Before signing, check the property register (등기부등본) and confirm the agent is licensed (공인중개사).

Confirm the full-option checklist

Before booking, verify: washer, fridge, A/C, cooker (gas or induction), desk, bed, and WiFi. Ask whether the maintenance fee (관리비) and utilities are separate from the quoted rent.

Winter gas heating is expensive

Korean apartments use gas floor heating (ondol). In January–February bills can run ₩100–200k extra per month. Ask who pays, and confirm whether the quoted rent covers gas in winter.
